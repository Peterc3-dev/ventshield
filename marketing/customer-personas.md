# VentShield — Customer Personas

## Persona 1: Maya — The Allergic Traveler

**Demographics**: 34, marketing manager, lives in Chicago, travels 6-8x/year for work and personal.

**Health**: Dust mite allergy, mild asthma. Carries an inhaler. Gets sinus headaches within hours of checking into hotels with poor air quality.

**Behavior**: Requests non-smoking rooms and higher floors. Has tried portable HEPA purifiers (too bulky and loud for hotel rooms). Has taped paper towels over vents in desperation. Googles "hotel room air quality tips" before every trip.

**Pain Point**: The portable purifier only cleans ambient air — the vent keeps pushing contaminated air directly at her. She needs source filtering, not room filtering.

**How She Finds VentShield**: Amazon search for "travel vent filter" or Instagram ad showing the snap-on demo.

**What Convinces Her**: seeing it installed on a ceiling vent in 10 seconds, the particle counter before/after data, and that it folds flat enough to fit in her laptop bag.

**Price Sensitivity**: Low. Already spends $150+ on a portable purifier. $30 travel kit is an easy buy. Will subscribe if it works.

## Persona 2: Darius — The Fed-Up Renter

**Demographics**: 27, software developer, rents a 1BR apartment in Miami. Has lived there 2 years.

**Health**: No diagnosed allergies but gets frequent sinus congestion and "apartment headaches" that go away when he's outside or at coffee shops.

**Behavior**: Noticed black residue around his ceiling vent louvers. Asked landlord about duct cleaning — was told it was done "before move-in" (no proof). Bought a cheap adhesive vent filter from Amazon — it fell off the ceiling vent within 48 hours. Considered calling code enforcement but doesn't want to antagonize his landlord.

**Pain Point**: He can't modify the HVAC system. He can't force a duct cleaning. He needs something that works on a ceiling vent without tools, adhesive, or landlord permission.

**How He Finds VentShield**: Reddit thread on r/mold or r/HomeImprovement. Someone posts a photo of their VentShield installed on a ceiling vent with a particle counter reading.

**What Convinces Him**: that it works on ceiling vents (magnets), that it's non-destructive (won't lose his deposit), and the color-change indicator so he can see it's actually doing something.

**Price Sensitivity**: Moderate. Will buy one to try ($18 frame + $12 filters). If it works, he'll filter 3 vents and subscribe.

## Persona 3: Angela — The Protective Parent

**Demographics**: 41, part-time nurse, two kids (ages 5 and 8), owns a 3BR house in Houston. Husband works oil and gas.

**Health**: Her 5-year-old has asthma. The pediatrician recommended improving indoor air quality. She replaced the main HVAC filter with a MERV 13 but her kid's bedroom vent still has visible dust buildup.

**Behavior**: Religiously changes the main HVAC filter every 60 days. Has a standalone HEPA purifier in the kids' room. Watches TikTok content about home health and mold awareness. Recently saw a viral video of someone opening their ductwork and finding black mold.

**Pain Point**: The main HVAC filter catches what goes into the system, but the ducts between the filter and the vent register are decades old and never cleaned. She wants point-of-delivery filtering at her kids' bedroom vents.

**How She Finds VentShield**: TikTok or Instagram ad. A demo video showing installation + particle counter results. Or a mommy blogger / pediatric health influencer recommendation.

**What Convinces Her**: antimicrobial treatment (specifically targeting mold), the MERV rating (she already understands MERV from her main filter), and the color-change indicator (proof it's working).

**Price Sensitivity**: Low for her kids' rooms. Will buy 2-3 frames immediately. Subscription is natural for her — she already auto-ships the main HVAC filter.
