### Competitor Weaknesses

1. **Adhesive failure**: primary complaint across all competitors. Gravity + humidity + HVAC vibration = filters falling off ceiling vents within days.
2. **No sizing system**: cut-to-fit wastes material and looks bad.
3. **No replacement signal**: users either replace too early (waste) or too late (ineffective).
4. **Not travel-oriented**: no one markets to travelers despite hotels being a major mold exposure vector.

## Target Customer Segments

### Segment 1: Allergy/Asthma Travelers
- Demographics: 25-55, income $50K+, travel 4+ times/year
- Pain: hotel rooms trigger symptoms, especially in humid climates
- Buying behavior: will pay premium for portable health solutions
- Channels: allergy forums, travel blogs, Amazon

### Segment 2: Mold-Aware Renters
- Demographics: 18-40, urban, apartment/condo dwellers
- Pain: can't modify HVAC, landlord won't address duct mold
- Buying behavior: looking for non-destructive, reversible solutions
- Channels: Reddit (r/mold, r/HomeImprovement), TikTok

### Segment 3: Health-Conscious Homeowners
- Demographics: 30-60, homeowners, aware of IAQ
- Pain: want supplemental filtering at individual vents beyond main HVAC filter
- Buying behavior: subscription-friendly, brand-loyal
- Channels: Amazon, HVAC forums, wellness influencers

### Segment 4: Temporary Housing
- Demographics: military families, disaster relief, transitional housing
- Pain: no control over HVAC in temporary quarters
- Buying behavior: institutional procurement possible
- Channels: direct B2B, government procurement

## Market Size Framework

### TAM (Total Addressable Market)
- US households: ~130M
- Households with central HVAC: ~90M
- Average vents per household: 8-12
- If every household filtered every vent: enormous, but unrealistic

### SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
- Households with someone who has allergies/asthma: ~50M (CDC estimate)
- Renters who can't modify HVAC: ~44M US renter households
- Hotel rooms in US: ~5.3M
- Overlap and realistic addressability: TBD

### SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
- Year 1 realistic: 5,000-20,000 units (DTC via Amazon + Shopify)
- Year 2 with traction: 50,000-100,000 units
- Breakeven volume: TBD (depends on tooling amortization)
